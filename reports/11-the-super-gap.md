# The Super Gap (초격차, Cho-gyeok-cha) - Kwon Oh-hyun

## Bottom line
- **What it is:** A management/leadership book by Kwon Oh-hyun (권오현), the former Vice Chairman and CEO of Samsung Electronics who ran its semiconductor business - distilling 33 years at Samsung into four pillars: **leader, organization, strategy, talent**. The title concept "초격차 / super gap" means building a lead so wide that rivals can't even conceive of competing.
- **Who should read it:** Operators, managers, and executives interested in how Samsung's semiconductor dominance was actually run - especially leaders thinking about org design, talent, and "innovation vs. improvement." Less useful as a startup go-to-market manual; it's large-company operating wisdom.
- **Time / access:** ~300+ pages, written to read easily. **CRITICAL CAVEAT: there is no verified official English translation.** The book is Korean-only (the Amazon listing is the Korean-language edition). English speakers face a hard reading barrier unless they read Korean or use machine translation.

## Author - confirmed
**Kwon Oh-hyun (권오현; English-romanized "Oh-Hyun Kwon"), former Vice Chairman & CEO of Samsung Electronics.** Verified details:
- Joined Samsung's semiconductor business in **1985** (US Samsung semiconductor research lab). Led development of the industry's first **64Mb DRAM in 1992**. ([web search of Wikipedia/Samsung Newsroom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kwon_Oh-hyun))
- Promoted to **Vice Chairman of Samsung Electronics in December 2011**; announced in **October 2017** he would step down as CEO (effective March 2018), citing an "unprecedented crisis." ([Samsung Global Newsroom](https://news.samsung.com/global/vice-chairman-oh-hyun-kwon-to-resign-as-ceo-of-samsung-electronics))
- Named to **TIME 100** (2013) and ranked highly on Harvard Business Review's best-performing-CEOs list. Under his leadership Samsung's semiconductor division reached record results; a Korean source states Samsung **overtook Intel to become the world's #1 semiconductor company in 2017**. ([Aladin / Korean retailer summaries](https://www.aladin.co.kr/shop/wproduct.aspx?ItemId=166283261))
- The book was **organized/co-written with Kim Sang-geun (김상근)**, who structured Kwon's recollections into the text. (listed as co-author on retailer pages, e.g. [Amazon Korean-edition listing](https://www.amazon.com/dp/8965706904))

## The "super gap" (초격차) concept
초격차 ("cho-gyeok-cha," literally "ultra-gap") is described as **refusing to be compared to any competitor** - raising the "격 (gyeok / level, class)" so high across *every* dimension that no one can challenge you. Crucially it is framed as more than a technology lead: it spans **technology, organization, systems, processes, talent placement, and culture.** It originated as a real Samsung strategy Kwon implemented to break out of a loss-making period in the semiconductor business. ([Korean retailer/press summaries](https://www.smedaily.co.kr/news/articleView.html?idxno=242005); [SME Daily](https://www.smedaily.co.kr/news/articleView.html?idxno=242005))

## Structure & the four pillars
The book is built around **four chapters** matching four keywords (sources consistently confirm this structure): ([Korean retailer/press summaries](https://www.smedaily.co.kr/news/articleView.html?idxno=242005))

1. **Leader (리더):** Argues that the ideal leader is the **"smart but lazy" type** - one who delegates and prioritizes rather than micromanaging. (Notably, press bios say Kwon himself started as a detail-obsessed micromanager and learned to grant autonomy as he rose.)
2. **Organization (조직):** Highly practical - redrawing the org chart from scratch, the craft of naming departments/teams, talent placement, and building performance-and-reward systems.
3. **Strategy (전략):** The substance of the "super gap" strategy - improvement vs. innovation, selection and concentration, and strategy for new businesses or turning around loss-making situations.
4. **Talent (인재):** Finding and cultivating talent who will drive future growth, and helping people grow themselves.

Length: **300+ pages**, described by Korean reviewers as easy to read with realistic, applicable advice. ([Korean retailer summaries](https://www.aladin.co.kr/shop/wproduct.aspx?ItemId=166283261))

### English availability - the key question
**No verified official English translation exists.** Searches of English book channels, Korean-literature-in-translation databases (LTI Korea, etc.), and the author/book name returned no English edition. The "Amazon.com" listing is the **Korean-language original** being sold internationally, not a translation; its title and metadata are Korean. **"The Super Gap" is a descriptive English rendering of the title, not the name of a published English book.** Bottom line: an English-only reader cannot read this book without translating it.

## Reception in Korea
- A major bestseller: reported at **200,000+ copies sold** in Korea. ([Korean search results citing "20만 베스트셀러"](https://www.aladin.co.kr/shop/wproduct.aspx?ItemId=166283261))
- Publicly recommended by Korean business figures (e.g., Shinsegae vice-chairman Chung Yong-jin reportedly chose it as holiday reading). ([Business Plus](https://www.businessplus.kr/news/articleView.html?idxno=31116))
- Strong enough to spawn a series: a follow-up **《초격차: 리더의 질문》 (2020, "The Super Gap: A Leader's Questions")** and a later **《다시, 초격차》 ("Again, The Super Gap")**. ([Kyobo listing for 다시, 초격차](https://product.kyobobook.co.kr/detail/S000219302492))

## Who it's for
Managers, executives, and leaders - especially those interested in **org design, talent systems, and strategy at scale**, and anyone wanting a first-hand account of how Samsung's semiconductor rise was operationally led. It's large-company operating wisdom from a chairman's seat rather than a startup/GTM playbook, so a go-to-market reader should expect leadership/organizational principles rather than tactical sales mechanics.
