## Flags worth knowing before you pick

- **#11 The Super Gap - likely unreadable for you.** No verified English translation exists; the book is Korean-only. High confidence, but not absolute. **Want me to dig harder for an English edition / equivalent?**
- **#07 Moral Economics & #05 Apple: The First 50 Years are brand new** (May 2026 and Mar 2026). Confirmed real, but reception is thin and a full-text read wasn't possible - reports rest on publisher copy, early reviews, and interviews.
- **#04 Apple in China "controversy":** Apple issued a blanket denial ("untrue / full of inaccuracies") to Vanity Fair, but I could **not** verify any formal published correction/retraction. Report says so plainly rather than asserting one.
- **Network caveat (all reports):** WebFetch was blocked for several primary domains, so some softer details come from cross-corroborated search snippets rather than full-page reads. Load-bearing facts were checked across multiple sources; each report's "Open questions" section is honest about what couldn't be pinned (exact page counts, verbatim chapter lists, a few attributed quotes).

## If I had to recommend a path (time is premium)

- **One book, pure enjoyment + a tech lesson:** #06 *How Music Got Free* or #10 *A Man for All Markets* - fast, narrative, and each carries a real idea (disruption; quantitative edge).
- **One book that maps to your crypto/markets work:** #08 *Why Nations Fail* (governance) or #09 *An Engine, Not a Camera* (reflexive, model-driven markets - harder but the deepest idea on the list).
- **A genuine mind-changer if you'll do the work:** #01 *How Life Works*.
- **Skip unless directly useful:** #02 (only pays off if you actually solve the problems), #11 (language barrier).
